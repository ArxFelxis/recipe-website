Ramen Noodle Soup Recipe

This savory ramen soup is easy to make, ready in a flash, and very, very good! I like to add ginger, soy sauce, sesame and chili oil for a delicious flavor.

Ramen noodle soup is a Japanese dish built on a savory broth, springy wheat noodles, and an array of toppings that balance comfort with complexity. The broth can be rich and creamy from pork bones (tonkotsu), light and salty with soy sauce (shoyu), or miso-based for earthy depth. Toppings often include slices of tender pork, a soft-boiled egg, seaweed, and scallions, each adding texture and flavor. At its best, ramen is both hearty street food and an art form of culinary craftsmanship.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 (3.5 ounce) package ramen noodles with dried vegetables
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon chili oil
  • ½ teaspoon minced fresh ginger root
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Combine broth and noodles in a medium saucepan; cover and bring to a boil over high heat, stirring to break up noodles.
  3. Reduce heat to medium and add soy sauce, chili oil, and ginger. Simmer, uncovered, for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in sesame oil.
  5. Garnish with green onions and serve.
